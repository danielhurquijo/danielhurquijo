Daniel Urquijo

I am a PhD student in political science at the European University Institute. I will be visiting the London School of Economics during Fall 2024 and Stanford University during Winter 2025. [CV]

I am interested in understanding how discriminatory laws come to be. While extensive research has highlighted their harmful effects on society, we still know surprisingly little about why such policies emerge in the first place. To tackle this question, I focus on two canonical examples of state-sponsored discrimination: apartheid in South Africa and Jim Crow in the American South. As part of my work, I am developing some exciting new datasets, including election results from South Africa (1910–1994), the complete set of laws passed by Southern states (1870–1920), and White area declarations during apartheid.

During my PhD I have helped organize the Comparative Politics Seminar Series and the Occassional Political Economy Seminar Series at the EUI. I was also a Teaching Assistant for the graduate-level Causal Inference course.

I hold a Bachelor's degree from the University of Liverpool and a Master's degree from King's College London, both in political economy.
